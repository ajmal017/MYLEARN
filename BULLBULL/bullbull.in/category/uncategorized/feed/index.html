One day all farmers and small towns folks will use smart or satellite phones, drive around on motorable roads in electric vehicles, and do commerce online.

Phone companies will sell phones, optic fibre cable companies will lay cables, infra companies will build roads, and software companies will build apps or online utilities.

Of course, to thrive in such an environment you must be neat and clean and have access to all the basics.

Included in the basics is the toilet/bathroom for all (specifically in villages and small towns).

Building toilets are included in the Swacch Bharat Abhiyan and so that angle is covered.

So what is the need for this article? And what is the multibagger sector we’re talking about?

Well, this article’s inspired by a chat I had with a couple of my journalist pals.

Running water is essential for all, and specifically for those living in very small towns and villages. Today toilets are being constructed but these are not equipped with running water.

Therefore, subsequent governments will have to spend on laying pipes if they truly want mofussil India to lead a quality life.

Now, we all know water is a scarce resource and that the day is not far when we will establish powerful desalination plants.

But we need water pipes to transport water from the desalination plant to cities and villages.

I’m sure the picture has become clear for you folks.

In the long term (say 4-5 years),Â  WATER PIPE companies will be on cloud 9 and if you invest in these today, you’ll end up very rich.

Now there are two kinds of water pipes – PVC and iron ductile. PVC pipes can be violated if they stretch for 100s of miles and therefore iron ductile pipes will be used for transporting water over long distances, while PVC pipes will be used for last mile transport.

Here is a list of companies that operate in this space. I have seen that these stocks are being picked up by strong hands. You too can judge by tracking the price and volume data.

Remember that you are responsible for all your financial decisions.

12th July, CNBC Hindi: Ashish Kukreja, a market expert, recommends Chartered Logistics at 20.80. He delivers a bullish commentary.

Watch his video here.

13th July: Chartered Logistics opens strong at 20.85 and jumps to 24 on strong volumes.

13 July, Corporate Announcements: Chartered Logistics discloses to the stock exchange/s that Mr Harsh Gandhi, their Executive Director, would be appearing on CNBC TV 18.

13 July, CNBC TV 18:Â Nigel D’souza interviews Mr Harsh Gandhi of Chartered Logistics, who engages in a bullish commentary about the company. Stock spurts from 23.25 to 24, a 3+% jump. It closes at 24.

13 July, Corporate Announcements: Chartered Logistics discloses to the stock exchange/s that ONGC has awarded 24-crore order which is to be executed in 2 years.

Too many coincidences.

Analysis of this Chartered Logistics Episode

  1. Is it possible that this whole episode was coordinated? That is, recommend on Hindi channel during off market hours on one day and interview the management the next day on the English channel?
  2. If this was unconnected, it implies that the guys at CNBC Hindi and English channels do not coordinate news and interviews.
  3. Is it possible that the recommending analyst, Chartered Logistics, and their PR firm are engaged in an effort to jack up stock prices? Remember that as per my information, PR firms are well connected to TV channels and they arrange interviews for their clients.
  4. If Chartered Logistics management informed the stock exchanges of the CNBC TV18 interview on 13 July, isn’t it obvious that the interview was fixed a few days earlier?
  5. Does not (4) above imply that the sequence of events tie up and look like a coordinated action?

To Conclude

The good thing about this whole thing is that Chartered Logistics is an okay kind of stock that reported an EPS of 1.4 for the year ended March 2017.

It will surely benefit from GST and its future will depend on how well it is managed.

One thing I’d advice you folks to do is:

Watch any one CNBC channel (Hindi or English) during market hours and the other during off market hours when stocks are being recommended. If what I have written above turns out to be a pattern, you can help yourself to many winners.
