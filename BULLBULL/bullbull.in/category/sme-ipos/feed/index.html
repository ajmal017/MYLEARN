Rajasthan-based home knick knack marker Priti International is issuing 7,00,800 at Rs 75. Is it worth applying to?

Find out in our unbiased review:

MOAT

Priti International makes small ticket home furnishing items and accessories, some furniture, pet comfort products, bags, and other personal goods. From the website it seems that it wants to expand into other home items, specially in furniture.

It is a decent line of business. Majority of the products are exported and the competition comes from the unorganized export sector and of course, from China.

At first glance, the company does radiate a decent perception, even though the moat is of average strength.

PAST

The company is new in the sense it has purchased the sole proprietorship company of the promoter for a consideration of 1.54 crores. So, there’s no point in going into the financials.

However…

…it is important to note that the company reported a negative operational cash flow for the period ended 31-12-17 and ended up financing its operations and investments from debt. This is because the company took over the business of the proprietor on 30 November 2017 for 1.54 crores, and that leaves a question – if the company was formed for taking over the business of the proprietor, who is now a promoter, why did it incur a cash outflow of Rs 1.96 crores from operations?

Now, it will be interesting to figure out how the company has priced the share at Rs 75.

IPO PRICING

The company justifies a price of Rs 75 a share because of

a. Experienced management

b. Global presence (but the company does not have offices overseas)

c. EPS of 3.47 on pre IPO capital. (this will further dilute post-IPO)

d. Return on Net Worth of 3.99% (Isn’t this a rather poor RoR?)

e. NAV of Rs 21 on 31-12-17

These are rather weak reasons for justifying an issue price of Rs 75.

Given these factors, and despite the company exuding a good perception, I would wait and see what happens post listing before deciding to enter or avoid Priti International.

Gujarat-based logistics company Accuracy Shipping is issuing 49.36 lakh shares at between Rs 81 and 84. The issue proceeds will be utilized for loan repayment,buying fleet and working capital requirements.

The company is considering a pre-IPO placement of 968000 shares. If this goes through, the shares issued to the public would be reduced to that extent.

Is the issue worth subscribing to? Find out in our unbiased review:

1.MOAT

Accuracy’s niche is that it focuses on granite and marble logistics (inland and exports). The company owns 150 vehicles and operates mainly in the Rajasthan, Gujarat and Maharashtra. It faces a lot of competition, and in that sense it is has a below-average moat.

However, one thing to note is that the company has been adding fleet every year and that is a good sign.

2. A CURIOUS CASE

Here’s a question:

If you own a company that requires massive working capital, and you have obtained a whole lot of finance in the last 5 years, faced negative cash flow issues in the past. own subsidiaries that are in losses, and work in an intensely competitive field, would you or other promoters float a firm that competes with the main company?

I don’t know what your answer to the question is, but Accuracy Shipping does all of the above activities that are listed in the question. Here’s the dope:

Negative Cash Flows

Intensive Working Capital Requirement

In house Competition

3. Financials

Despite the slowdown in exports, the company has clocked a massive rise in turnover for the 9-month period ending on 31-12-17.

It’s book value was 21 as on 31-12-17.

I’d wait for the stock to list before considering trading it. Smallcaps too are falling and many are available at very attractive prices. In such a situation it is best to be a fence sitter.

Gujarat based family run business, Sonam Clock, is issuing 28.08 lakh shares at Rs 36 per share.

Is it worth applying to?

Find out in our no-holds barred review.

BUSINESS AND MOAT

The company is into the corporate gifting business. It makes clocks in bulk for companies wishing to give these away as mementos for any occasion.

By and large, the company imports clock stuff (fancy designs, etc.), assembles these here and brands them with their client’s logo. Exports make up about 40% of sales (2017).

The promoter’s in the clock business since 32 years, and that’s a positive sign.

The company is well entrenched in the market and so long corporates keep gifting clocks, it will do well.

QUIRKY VEHICLE LOANS

Promoters buy cars in their personal name and company pays the loans. Shouldn’t the title be in the company’s name? Though the amount is not significant, the accounting practice is not desirable.

REPAYMENT OF LOAN TO PROMOTER

One of the objectives of the issue is to repay a 2 crore loan to the promoter.

So if I have a 32 years of experience in a company, run a successful business and own an established brand, and have given a 2 crore loan to my company, why would I get it repaid through an IPO? Wouldn’t I rather be repaid with retained earnings and use the IPO proceeds for expanding business?

NEGATIVE CASH FLOWS

An established brand and business with a promoter with decades of experience is expected to generate positive cash flows at the operational level.

IN HOUSE COMPETITION

The promoters own another company that freely competes with the one issuing the shares. Conflict of interest always weakens any business.

BASIS FOR PRICING

The weighted average of the EPS BEFORE the IPO is 1.28. This will bet diluted post issue. The company is issuing shares at Rs 36, which results in a PE (pre IPO) of 28. Post IPO the PE will be higher.

The issue is at a high price.

CONCLUSION

IMO the issue is priced very high and therefore I would give it a MISS.

Gujarat based Waa Solar is issuing 20 lakh shares at Rs 161 per share. Of this 17.5 lakh shares make for a fresh offer while 2.5 lakh shares are being offered by existing shareholders.

Is the issue worth applying to? Find out in this unbiased review.

COMPLIANCE ISSUES

The company has given 23.5 crore loans to group companies and relatives of directors and in addition has given a guarantee of 325 crores on behalf of a loan obtained by another group company Both acts are not in compliance of the Companies Act, and therefore a red flag.

Additionally, the company has pledged its investment of 93,90,600 Shares in a subsidiary company, Madhav Karnataka (Solar) Private limited, with lenders of 63 Crores to that subsidiary company. This is in compliance of all laws, though.

OVERSTATED RESERVES

The company has changed its method of accounting depreciation from Written Down Value to Straight Line method. This has inflated its reserves by about 43.5 crores.

Now, here’s what’s really interesting. See this image.

The total reserves are 115.20 crores, of which 43.5 crores are because of the change in the depreciation method, and the rest of the reserves is represented by share premium. This implies that retained earnings are negative (115.20 crores less 43.5 crores = 72 crores which is lesser than the share premium of Rs 75.34 crores).

CASH FLOWS

Though the cash flows are positive there’s a whole lot of inconsistency. For example, the company has generated operating cash of 22.37 crores for the year ended 31-3-17, but for next 8 months, the operational cash is a negative 92 lakhs. It’s very inconsistent.

BUSINESS AND OTHER FACTORS

The company seems to have a working business model having won contracts from the Gujarat and Punjab governments. From what I saw on the website, the business looks good.

CONCLUSION

Give the ballooning up of reserves because of the change in the accounting method, the non compliance of statutory provisions and the relatively high Debt:Equity ratio (88 crore debt and about 6.7 crore post issue equity capital on consolidated basis), and the fact that other solar companies are not too hot, I would give this issue a MISS.

The profit for 8 months ended 30 November 2017 was 89 lakhs, so let’s say the profit for the whole year would work up to Rs 1.4 crores.

That leaves us with an EPS of Re 0.85.

The IPO is priced at a PE of 30.5, which is very aggressive.

CONCLUSION

All in all, this looks like a decent IPO except for the fact it is priced high. There are insignificant legal issues, the promoters have bought the shares at par, the company is in the ever-growing food business, etc., etc.

I would buy the stock in the open market if it falls to Rs 12-15.

Pune based Affordable Robotic & Automation is issuing 36.67 lakh shares at Rs 75 premium. Is the issue worth subscribing to? Here’s an unbiased review:

MOAT:

The company operates a robotics facility (machines/software) that offers turnkey automated solutions (design and prototype) for the automotive industry, and it also creates car parking solutions  for clients in Pune and Mumbai (as in complex car parking systems that you see in malls).

Basically the company gets an order, makes some prototypes, gets the customer’s sign off and installs the required paraphenalia at the customer’s plant. It can even set up a new facility for customers.

It is headed by an experienced technocrat, Milind Padole.

For designing parts, the company uses licensed software (it does not own any in-house developed software).

It has collaborations in China, Taiwan and Netherlands, and their PDF also owns a facility in China. It services reputed clients such as Maruti, Volvo, Eicher, M&M, Tata, Ashok Leyland, etc.

One new and very exciting business that the company has entered into is the secondary packaging business which  involves picking up used plastic bottles, refurbishing these, and refilling with water, etc. This type of recycling business has fantastic potential given the problem of plastic waste we face.

Note that the automation business is growing rapidly in India and the space is getting very competitive. Though the company’s business model looks good especially considering EVs still have to take off, we can say that the moat is above average for now, and its strength will depend on what the company does going forward.

TAX ISSUES:

The company has faced tax issues in the past and has an outstanding demand of Rs 9.55 crore towards direct and indirect taxes. In March 2018, the company paid 3.32 crores and from that date, Rs 6.23 crores may be outstanding. There is no clarity on the current situation, and time will provide the answers.

ERRATIC CASH FLOWS:

As you can see in the image, the company has experienced solid spurts and declines in the cash flows. The reason for this could be the fact that though the company was in automation operations since 2005, the actual work started from 2010. Later, it had to invest in car parking solutions, which now contribute 19% of total turnover. This may have squeezed cash flows.

PRICE TO BOOK VALUE:

The company’s BV is 17 and the issue price is Rs 85, making it 5 times book, which seems expensive.

PROMOTER’S COST OF ACQUISITION

The promoters have acquired the stock at 40 paise. Well, this is a normal with all SME IPOs and the low cost of acquisition represents the reward to the promoters for their dynamism. So this should not be a factor.

INVENTORIES TO SALES:

The inventories work up to to 54% of sales, implying that many orders were in process. Therefore, one factor that needs to be checked post-IPO is whether sales increased.

UTILIZATION OF ISSUE PROCEEDS:

From the looks of it, the company would be able to easily resolve its debt and working capital requirements post-issue, which is a good sign.

ISSUE PRICING:

The weighted EPS for the last 3 years works out to Rs 3.85 pre IPO. You can say that the issue is fully priced. The NAV pre-IPO is 17.54 (31-3-17).

COMPETITION:

According to the prospectus, the company has no listed competitors and therefore peer comparison isn’t possible.

CONCLUSION:

Though it seems fully priced, Affordable Robotics & Automation seems to have that X factor that most SME issues lack. The new business of refilling also seems very exciting. The company looks like a medium-long term horse to bet on. Plus, there are high chances of listing gains as well.
