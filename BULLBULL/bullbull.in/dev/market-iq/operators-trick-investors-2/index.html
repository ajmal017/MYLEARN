How Operators Trick Investors – 2

TOPICS:how indian stock markets workhow operators game investors

Posted By: sunil.tinani October 11, 2017

I’ll explain what happened with the help of screenshots.

Stock monitored was Gravita Industries.

The key here is that many investors view the book before making buying or selling decisions and some operators take advantage of that.

Gravita opened today at around 169 and quickly 171.

At around 10 AM I opened Gravita’s book and saw that there were about 8,00,000 buyers and 2,20,000 sellers.

15-20 minutes later the price dropped to 169. The number of buyers and sellers were 7,00,000 and 2,40,000 respectively.

Now Gravita is a fancied stock and any eager investor may have bought the stock around 170 levels after viewing the book.

In another 30-40 minutes or so (I do not recollect the time, but you can check the timestamp on my screenshots), the price dropped to 165-166 but the buyers still outnumbered sellers. Over time, the price kept dropping until the price was between 163-164. At these levels, the buyers and sellers were almost equal in number.

Please refer to the images and check the captions

Though I started monitoring the stock from 170 levels, I did not grab the screen. Here is the situation when the price was around 165. You can see the buyers outnumber the sellers, giving a bullish signal for investors who follow the book.

By about 12 PM, the price had dropped to 163 levels and yet the buyers outnumbered the sellers.

The same story continued even at 2.25 PM by which time the price had dropped to 157 levels.

The stock closed around 156.90.

Conclusion

If you invest in stocks by viewing the book, then keep the following in mind:

If buyers outnumber sellers and the price continues to drop — then it implies there is either a hidden seller or a big seller who has not registered a sell transaction. You should get out of such stocks that exhibit such price movements within an hour of getting into the trade. However,, this does not translate to a full blown bearish signal especially if you know that the stock is high quality. It represents operator selling.

Conversely, if sellers outnumber buyers and the price continues to rise — it implies there is either a hidden buyer or a big buyer who has not registered a buy transaction. This is an extremely bullish signal. If you come across such stocks, you should immediately buy them.
