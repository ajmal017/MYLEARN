The Stock Axis Mobile App – A Stock Picker’s Delight

TOPICS:stock axis appstock axis app reviewed

Posted By: sunil.tinani June 5, 2017

There are not enough words to describe the greatness of the Stock Axis mobile app, and I really cannot stop raving about how helpful it is.

What information does a fundamental analyst need?

  1. Market Cap
  2. PE Ratio
  3. Enterprise Value
  4. P/BV
  5. Shareholding breakup
  6. EV/EBITDA
  7. Quarterly and annual results (P/L + B/S)
  8. Cash Flow statement
  9. Financial Ratios
  10. Peer Comparison
  11. News

Well, the Stock Axis gives him all this information in a jiffy

Stock Axis even covers the EV/EBITDA number

The Enterprise to EBITDA is a recent parameter that is being increasingly used by analysts to value companies that have solid growth potential but are running into losses because they have made investments in growth assets.

In such companies, the PE ratio naturally cannot apply.

For example, Aditya Birla Fashion was trading between 130-150 for a long time at a EV:EBITDA of 20 even though it reported losses. It has just just turned the corner and reported a small profit and it’s stock price has jumped to 180.

The lower the EV/EBITDA number, the more attractive the growth stock becomes.

The Stock Axis App informs researchers on this crucial number.

The app also analyzes stocks from  the fundamental and technical points of view

The Stock Axis guys have created some sort of a proprietary algorithm that allots a number between 1 and 99 to each listed stock.

The higher the number, the more potential the stock has of scaling new heights.

However, I would urge all equity investors not to follow this number blindly and consult their broker before making a BUY decision.

This article illustrates how the Stock Axis app’s trend rating can go horribly wrong MAINLY because it uses AI (Artificial Intelligence) to evaluate the fundamentals and the technicals and allot a rating to each stock.

The only thing that is missing from the Stock Axis app is…

And that is the commodity data. Prices of raw material, demand and supply scenario, competition, future prospects, obsolescence potential.

But I guess all this data cannot be had for free.

You can get the app here.  And no, this is not a promotional article, the StockAxis app is genuinely fantastic and I’m a fan.

Go right ahead and use it, and happy researching.
