Before rushing to act on this information, know this:

  1. That this portfolio is as on 31 March 2017. Let me give you an example of the importance of time (though you can never time a buy or sell to perfection):Here is the price chart of Geojit, stock that Rakesh has purchased for the quarter ended 31 March 2017.

    The first quarter of March 2017 represents the period between 1 January 2017 and 31 March 2017.

    At that time, Geojit was hovering around 50 bucks, which is the price at which Rakesh would have entered. At the time of writing this article, its price is 83.

    Now it is possible that even though Rakesh is a long term investor, he may unload Geojit if he senses senseless euphoria or a market crash. He would have made his money and if you have purchased at these levels, you’d be left holding the baby.

    So, be cautious, it’s your money.

  2. Do not act on this blog post. It is mere information. Talk to your financial advisor/broker and use your judgment.
  3. All the information published here is in public domain, all you have to do is search for it.
