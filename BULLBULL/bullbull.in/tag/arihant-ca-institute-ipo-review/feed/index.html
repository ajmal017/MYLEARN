Gujarat based CA, CS and ICAI training institute, Arihant Institute, is issuing 25 lakh shares at Rs 30 to finance its expansion. Is it worth applying to? Find out in this unbiased review.

MOAT

Post GST, RERA, demonetization, etc., the CA business is on a roll and therefore it is natural to assume that Arihant is in a growing market. It also seems to enjoy a good brand image in Gujarat where it operate a 20000 sft institute and employs several professionals, plus it has franchised out branches too.

It is not known about the competition that the company faces because tutorials are not a regulated business. Moreover, bulk of the operations are localized in Ahmedabad.

It will be safe to say that Arihant has a below-average moat.

CASH FLOWS

The company has experienced negative cash flows right up to 31-1-18. This is a red flag because an established institute that says it has a super brand value must report positive operational cash flows.

COMPLIANCE ISSSUES

The company has not complied with the ROC filings and its directors have been disqualified.

Though non compliance does not result in material financial penalties, it does reflect badly on the company.

TRADE RECEIVABLES

Typically a training institute collects fees in advance.

In Arihant’s case, its trade receivables work up to 2.18 crores and its sales are 1.14 crores.

Of the total debtors, Arihant claims 88 lakhs are retained by its franchisees, but are considered good.

The balance debtors work up to 1.3 crores (roughly) and the sales are 1.14 crores, which implies the institute allows a credit period of over 12 months to its students. It is also not clear whether the 1.14 crore sales include franchise sales.

ALSO: The reserves and surplus item is negative.

CONCLUSION

At this moment, I have seen enough to suggest this issue is an AVOID. Those of you who are interested should read through the entire prospectus and chat with the management before applying.
