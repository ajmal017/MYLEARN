This is a step by step guide. Let’s get started:

GENERAL RULES

  1. Traders MUST read this article before jumping into a trade. Know that once you know the big picture and the short term trend, you can use 15m and 30m candles to enter into a trade.
  2. The VWAP generally equalizes with the CMP during the day, and that is the time a trader should act. If the price starts moving above the VWAP, it is bullish, and the VWAP will gradually move up to equalize with the price over the space of a few candles.
  3. Investors should work with 1-hour or 1-Day charts. 1-Day charts can provide better medium term cues, while 1-hour charts provide the trend for a swing trade.
  4. Use charts only for FNO stocks and Indices. Do not track illiquid stocks on charts because these can be manipulated easily.
  5. This trade setup will give you about 2-3 trades on the Indices every week, and no more. For stocks, it is up to you as it depends on the number of stocks you are interested in.

UNDERSTANDING CHART TIME FRAMES 

A trader or investor typically checks 3 time frames:

  1. ONE WEEK for understanding overall trend (time period to check – 4-5 months)
  2. ONE DAY for understanding short term trends (time period to check – 5-6 weeks)
  3. ONE HOUR for entering into trades (time period to check – 3-4 weeks)

SETTING UP THE TRADE (Updated on 31-5-18)

  1. Log in at Investing.com
  2. Search for any symbol or index.
  3. Set up the following indicators:
    a. SuperTrend (7, 3): It alerts you when the trend changes by plotting arrows on the chart.
    b. VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price): Bullish when price moves above it, and bearish otherwise.
    c. Ichimoku Cloud: Read this post to figure out how it works.
    d. MACD: Earlier, I worked with the Chaikin Oscillator but have found that the MACD gives a more reliable signal. Read this article to learn how the MACD should be interpreted.
    e. I have added 13-DAY and 48-DAY EMAs to the setup. When 13D EMA crosses the 48D EMA it is a bullish signal, and vice versa.
    f. Note that the images below do not contain MACD and the EMAs, but even without those, the setup works perfectly.

INTERPRETATION

Check the numbers on the chart before reading this interpretation:

  1. Supertrend signaled a buy on 16-4-18 at 10488-10500 levels. At this point, the priced moved ferociously above the VWAP, another bullish signal. As you can observe, the VWAP (the thick blue line) moved up and equalized with the price after a few candles. Also, at this time the Ichimoku conversion line was above the base line, and the price moved above the conversion line, and so, we had a bullish trend confirmed by 3 signals, while the Chaikin Oscilltor suggested that money was moving out. However, the Chaikin Oscillator keeps evening out over a period of time, and as I recall, it was trending up when the other 3 signals suggested bullishness.
  2. The Nifty hit a high of 10,552 and started dropping (see the big red candle).
  3. Though the Supertrend indicator was still positive, the price started putting pressure on the VWAP and on the Ichimoku Conversion line (the thin red line) and that suggested that it was time to book profits and stay on the sidelines. The Chaikin Oscillator also indicated that money was dumping the Nifty (#5).
  4. The support for the Nifty existed at 10506 (the green Supertrend line)  and 10460 (the first Ichimoku cloud line).
  5. This is explained in #3 above.

INTERPRETATION REVIEW

It is 11.45 AM now, and I am reviewing the interpretation written at 11 AM. Here is the chart:

  1. At 11.45 the scenario has played out as per the interpretation above. At this time, the Supertrend is still bullish. On the bearish side, the Chaikin Oscilltor has seen substantial volume selling, the prices are below the VWAP and  the Ichimoku Conversion Line.
  2. The Chaikin Oscillator also has flattened out and the last candle shows a rejection of selling. It’s 11.53 now and I see that the next candle is green, and the Chaikin Oscilltor has started rising. So at this point, I would wait to see how the price moves in relation to the VWAP and then make a decision. So, the strategy now is to sit on the sidelines and wait for a signal.
  3. Know that the 1-day Nifty Candle chart is in bullish zone and therefore any shorts should not me taken home UNLESS the 1D Chart reverses.
