How The Stock Axis App Can Go Horribly Wrong
Rate this post

Though the Stock Axis app is a researcher’s delight, it uses AI to evaluate a stock’s fundamental and technical indicators and then goes on to rate the stock based on what the AI reports.

The rating is represented by a number between 1 and 99 and the higher the number, the more investable a share is.

Stock Axis also predicts the direction of the stock’s price based on this rating by allotting a UP or a DOWN to each stock.

Unless you are an experienced stock investor, you must not use the app’s rating factor because it can be wrong.

Let me give you an example:

I’ll talk about my favorite stock (at the moment) — Gujarat Pipavav Port Ltd.

Take a look at what the Stock Axis App has predicted:

Their algorithm suggests that Gujarat Pipavav is a weak stock (rating 42/99) and is on a downward trend.

If you follow this AI rating blindly, you will sell off Gujarat Pipavav without thinking twice.

On 16 June 2017, the share shot up to 153, thereby proving conclusively that the Stock Axis  App’s rating and trend were horribly wrong.

To sum up, my advice to you is not to follow any AI rating blindly.

The stock Axis app is extremely useful when it comes to analyzing fundamentals, but it’s AI rating should be ignored. Instead, use your own judgment.
