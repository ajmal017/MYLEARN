Nakoda Group of Industries IPO Review

Posted By: sunil.tinani May 21, 2018

Nakoda Group of Industries IPO Review
4 (80%) 1 vote

Nakoda Group, a food processing company, is issuing 18.5 lakh shares at Rs 35. It will be listed on BSE SME platform. Is it worth applying to? Find out in our unbiased review:

MOAT:

The company is engaged in:

a. Food processing (pulp, jelly beans, canned foods) – It is not clear whether the company sells under its own brand so it will be safe to assume that it supplies to large manufacturers.

b. Branded Products: The company owns a brand “Frootos” (jams and candies). Unsure about how the brand does, but if it were doing well, the share premium would have been greater.

c. Kirana Item Trading: The company sources spices and other kirana items and either repackages these or sells in their brand name. This is not clear.

d. Almond/Dry Fruit Imports: The company imports almonds, processes them, and either repackages these or sells in their brand name. This is not clear.

Though the food industry is expected to do go well going forward, the company’s fortunes depend on how well it markets its brand (Frootos, Nakoda). The company’s headquartered in Maharashtra and therefore expanding pan India will take massive marketing spend.

Also, competitions like Dabur, HUL and others own established brands and it will be an almost impossible task to breath through their defenses. But it is possible that Nakoda’s food processing business does well in the future. Main clients are food packaging companies, bakeries, eateries, etc.

Nakoda’s moat is not formidable.

FINANCIALS

A. Business

Of the total business:

46% is represented import and sale of almonds (so is US $ advances, the company will get shafted)

30% comes from trading (a low margin business)

24% comes from food processing (of this 55% is exported)

B. IN-HOUSE COMPETITION

When a high quality group enters a business, the idea is to knock the socks off the competition and curbing conflict of interest. In Nakoda’s case, other companies belonging to the promoter group shall continue to compete with the issuing company, a red flag.

B. LOW NET PROFIT MARGINS

The company made a meager 3% net profit on sales for the year ended 31-3-17, and the profit has resulted because of inventory level adjustment.

B. DEBT-EQUITY-IPO FUNDS

The company’s equity pre issue is 5 crores. The long term debt is 6.2 crore and the short term debt is 9.35 crores.

The IPO proceeds will be utilized to buy plant and machinery (1.5 crore) and fund working capital (3.5 crore).

Then the company says that it regularly needs a hell of a lot of working capital to keep the business machine moving.

The question that arises is how will the company effectively service debt or repay it while working with a very low net profit margin?

CONCLUSION

Given the numbers (low profit margins), debt situation, in-house competition, effort required to build brand, and the weak moat, I would AVOID this issue.

I stopped the review after reading up to the financials, and made my decision then. Those of you who are yet interested should read through the entire prospectus and make a decision.
